While a weekend sleep-in might restore subjective feelings of alertness and temporarily clear adenosine (sleep pressure), it does not reverse the metabolic, cardiovascular, or cognitive damage incurred during the week. Chronic sleep debt alters baseline genetic expression, which a weekend cannot reset. Furthermore, shifting wake times on weekends causes "social jetlag," disrupting the circadian rhythm for Monday.

Caffeine is an adenosine receptor antagonist. Throughout the day, a chemical called adenosine builds up in the brain, creating "sleep pressure." Caffeine molecules are structurally similar to adenosine and physically block the brain's adenosine receptors. It does not create energy; it blocks the signal of exhaustion. Once the caffeine metabolizes (half-life of 5-7 hours), all the built-up adenosine floods the receptors simultaneously, causing the infamous "caffeine crash."

However, the brain requires deep NREM (Non-Rapid Eye Movement) sleep for physical repair and memory consolidation. Polyphasic sleep severely deprives the brain of NREM stages. Ultimately, practitioners are existing in a constant state of severe sleep deprivation and survival-mode microsleeps, which is unsustainable and medically dangerous long-term.
